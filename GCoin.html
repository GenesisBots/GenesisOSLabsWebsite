What is Gcoin?

Genesis Coin (GC) is GenesisOS Labs’ native tokenization asset on Algorand. It is issued as an Algorand Standard Asset (ASA) and positioned as the L2 tokenization layer — public, AI auditable, ready to tokenize product & customer workflows.

ASA

Algorand L2 Asset

GCoin is an ASA—native to Algorand’s asset framework for minting, transfers, payments, & tokenization.

Ledger

Public ledger

Balances and transfers settle on GCoin’s public ledger, so auditors and partners can verify activity without trusting a private database alone.

DEX

Tinyman pool live today

A Tinyman liquidity pool is funded and active on Algorand mainnet, giving GCoin public price discovery & swap access.

Internal

Tokenize GenesisOS products

Internally, GCoin rails can represent product lifecycle events, workflow completions, and automation accounting across GenesisOS & Aerys Desktop modules.

External

Customer ecosystems

Externally, customers can adopt GCoin for shared on-chain workflows—or choose private / hybrid tokenization models described below.

Why Algorand

Algorand is a Layer‑1 blockchain designed for fast, secure, scalable settlement. GCoin inherits those properties through the ASA framework and Pure Proof‑of‑Stake consensus.

Pure Proof‑of‑Stake

Consensus participates through stake, not energy‑intensive mining—aligned with enterprise sustainability and predictable network behavior.

High throughput

Algorand is built for high transaction volume so tokenized workflows and agent handoffs can scale with GenesisOS automation.

Low fees

Low, predictable fees make frequent mint, burn, and transfer events practical for operational and accounting use—not only speculative trading.

Instant finality

Blocks finalize in seconds with cryptographic finality, so workflow verification and settlement do not wait on probabilistic confirmations.

Enterprise‑grade security

Public ledger transparency plus Algorand’s security model give compliance and IT teams a clear audit surface for tokenized processes.

Inside GenesisOS, GCoin is the default public rail for turning product activity into auditable on-chain records. Tokenization is optional—you choose when product and legal review are complete.

Mint, burn, track, audit

Internal workflows can mint and burn units, track balances, and retain audit trails that reconcile automation logs with on-chain state.

Automation & accounting

Use GCoin rails for workflow verification, product lifecycle tracking, and accounting hooks that sit beside your existing ERP or finance stack.

Agents & workflows

GBot Agents and GenesisOS workflows can trigger token events at approved gates—so humans stay in control while automation keeps records current.

Customer Tokenization Options

Customers are not locked into a single model. GenesisOS supports public GCoin, your own public tokens, private minting, and hybrid designs.

1

Use GCoin (public ASA)

Adopt Genesis Coin on Algorand for shared liquidity, public transparency, and integration with GenesisOS product rails.

2

Your own public token

Issue or connect your own public token on any ledger your partners already use—Algorand ASA or other networks supported by GenesisOS settlement paths.

3

Private token (no market price)

Have GenesisOS mint a private token for internal accounting and pilots—useful when you need auditability without public market price discovery.

4

Hybrid models

Combine internal private rails with public GCoin or external tokens—for example, private operational tokens plus public settlement or liquidity.

Tinyman Pool

GCoin’s first DEX liquidity pool is funded and active on Tinyman, Algorand’s decentralized trading protocol. Liquidity providers and swappers interact with a public pool whose reserves and trades are visible on-chain.

Liquidity

The pool holds paired assets so users can swap into or out of GCoin. Deeper liquidity supports smoother operational and market activity.

Public transparency

Pool state lives on Algorand’s public ledger—reserves, swaps, & LP activity can be inspected independently of GenesisOS Labs’ application databases.
